title: Basement remodel...does this count as a remodel?
---

Over the course of 2020 and 2021, I re-built the basement of our house to be a better living space year-round. I have never done a remodel before and most of the tasks that I did, I did for the very first time. It has taken countless hours to complete and there were parts of it I enjoyed more then other parts but overall, it has been a great project I am very proud of. If I had to do it again, I would have done some things differently for sure but I would indeed do it again. 

# Why did I start this project? 

We moved into our 1950 home in 2018. A basement, main floor, and a half story on top. We love our house 😄. Only problem, our finished basement was a concrete basement with no insulation. In the winter time, our basement used to reach 52 degrees fahrenheit and that's with the furnace heating it up! We only used it during the summer months and that really bummed us out. 

March 2020 came and the world went on lock-down for covid. During this time alone at our house, we got bored and decided to do some house projects we wanted to get to. We rented a dumpster and tore down an old shed in the backyard. We had some extra room left in the dumpster so we thought, *let's tear down the basement and throw that in the dumpster!* Without putting more thought into starting a huge project like re-doing a basement from scratch, we started tearing down the basement. 

The end-goal was to simply have an insulated basement and make it a warm space we could use year-round. Spoiler: We have accomplished that goal! I have been using it daily all winter long 😄

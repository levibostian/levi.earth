I am all about the experience. coffee on a saturday morning alone. bathroom before eating a meal. the drive is just as important as the destination in a road-trip. 

Friends and family laugh when they see me working on my tiny little details when being around them. saying, "you are all about the experience". 

Then it hit me. No wonder I am in love with user experience of using a product. The landing page, the copy, the docs, how I am obsessed with not using crappy error messages, customer support. It's all about the experience! From start to finish, it all matters. It all comes together. People are going through an emotional experience when they use what you make. I build things because I want others to use it. I want them to feel what I want them to feel. 

Experience may not matter to everyone. Not everyone appreciates the crafts-person-ship that the creator put into something. However, it's one way that can make your product go from something someone likes to something someone loves. Experience matters. 

Experience is user-experience (UX). The UX design of the actual software product is part of the full experience, yes. But if you have crappy docs or crappy customer support, the UX doesn't matter. It gets ignored. 

Sure, this conversation can get into perfectionism. But that's for a different day :)
